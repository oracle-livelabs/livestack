# Scene 2 Data Foundation

## Introduction

**Data Foundation** shows why the rest of the demo can tell a connected clinical-supply story. The operational signal is fragmented data: regulated products, quality signals, trial sites, supply orders, inventory, routes, analytics, and audit activity need to be understood together before a team can make a defensible decision.

In a real life sciences environment, those signals usually originate in specialized systems such as CTMS, RTSM/IRT, EDC, QMS, manufacturing, depot, logistics, and regulatory platforms. Seer Lifesciences is framed as the governed intelligence layer that connects those signals for decision-making. It does not replace the systems that create or manage the transactions.

On this page, the connected dataset is validated before the risk journey continues. Oracle AI Database supports the layer by keeping relational records, JSON documents, graph relationships, spatial data, vector matches, model outputs, and audit records close enough for governed analytics and explainable follow-up.

## Task 1: Prepare the dataset

![Data Foundation page with the Restore Demo Data button and record counts highlighted](images/data-foundation.png)

Use this page to confirm that the operational evidence is ready before continuing through the demo.

1. Click **Data Foundation** in the sidebar.
2. In **Prepare the Dataset**, review the live record counts.
3. If the dataset needs to be reset, click **Restore Demo Data**.
4. Wait for the progress indicator to finish and review the refreshed counts.

In the current demo dataset, the page shows **14,622** tracked records, including **79** regulated products, **5,000** quality signals, **3,000** clinical supply orders, **79** product vectors, **5,000** signal vectors, and **1,464** semantic matches. The decision this supports is readiness: the risk journey has enough product, signal, order, and analytic evidence to be credible.

**Note:** Sample values may change after data refreshes or rebuilds. Verify live output before relying on specific sample values.

## Task 2: Review what gets loaded

![Data Foundation page with What Gets Loaded carousel highlighted](images/data-foundation.png)

The loaded domains explain how the demo can move from signal to governed action without requiring separate data tools.

1. Scroll to **What Gets Loaded**.
2. Read the three visible carousel tiles.
3. Use the right carousel arrow to review the remaining tiles.
4. Look for the major evidence types used in the demo: regulated supply core records, quality and regulatory signals, graph relationships, spatial coverage, vector embeddings, machine learning outputs, and agent audit records.

The business point is that a clinical supply or quality leader needs a connected evidence layer before deciding whether to investigate a signal, protect inventory, adjust supply planning, or open a governed follow-up action.
