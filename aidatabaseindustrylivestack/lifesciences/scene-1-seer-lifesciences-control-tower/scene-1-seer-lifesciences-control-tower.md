# Scene 1 Welcome and Demo Orientation

## Introduction

**Welcome** frames the demo as one connected regulated supply story. The business question is simple: when a quality, regulatory, logistics, or trial-site signal appears, can the organization quickly understand exposure, decide what needs attention, and create a reviewable follow-up path?

Life sciences teams often receive signals in one place while product, trial-site, order, depot, route, and audit evidence sit in separate specialized systems. That fragmentation makes it harder for sponsors, CROs, clinical supply teams, quality leaders, and manufacturing partners to protect trial continuity without slowing the business down.

This page frames Seer Lifesciences as an operational intelligence layer above those systems. Oracle AI Database supports the story by connecting multiple data shapes and AI-assisted workflows in one governed decision layer, while the opening message stays business-led: detect risk earlier, connect evidence faster, and act with traceability.

Estimated Time: **5 minutes**

In this scene, you will learn how the demo frames a clinical-supply continuity story, what evidence the later pages connect, and how to move into the guided flow.

## Task 1: Review the clinical supply story

![Welcome page with the eight-use-case clinical supply story highlighted](images/control-tower.png)

Use the welcome page to establish the end-to-end journey: signal detection, exposure assessment, investigation, operational impact, decision, and auditable action.

1. Click **Welcome** in the sidebar.
2. Review **Eight use cases, one clinical supply risk story** in the top panel.
3. Read the eight numbered use case summaries. They connect quality signals, regulated products, cold-chain coverage, clinical supply orders, analytics, and governed action into one operating view.
4. Review the visible **Key Life Sciences Use Cases Featured** tiles.

The framing should stay business focused. Lead with clinical supply continuity, quality traceability, regulatory readiness, and governed action rather than feature-by-feature technology detail.
